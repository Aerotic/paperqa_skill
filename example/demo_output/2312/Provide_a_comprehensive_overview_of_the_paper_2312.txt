=== ANSWER ===
MobileGPT is an LLM-based system for mobile task automation that addresses the non-deterministic nature of LLMs by augmenting them with human-like app memory (lee2024mobilegptaugmentingllm pages 11-12). The key problem is that LLMs lack reliable task recall and struggle with repeated tasks due to their inherent non-determinism (lee2024mobilegptaugmentingllm pages 11-12).

The proposed methodology uses a multi-phase approach: during cold-start, MobileGPT goes through **Explore**, **Select**, and **Derive** phases to learn new tasks; during warm-start, it uses sub-task slot-filling and in-context adaptation for recalling learned tasks (lee2024mobilegptaugmentingllm pages 11-12). The system employs a hierarchical memory architecture that groups app screens into 'nodes' based on shared sub-tasks, enabling efficient task reproduction and cross-task sharing (lee2024mobilegptaugmentingllm pages 7-8). A dual-strategy failure handling mechanism includes self-correcting via feedback generation and human-in-the-loop task repair (lee2024mobilegptaugmentingllm pages 5-6). MobileGPT uses GPT-4-turbo for screen understanding and GPT-3.5-turbo for slot-filling (lee2024mobilegptaugmentingllm pages 8-9).

Experimental evaluation uses a custom dataset of 80 tasks across 8 apps with two instructions each (160 total), comparing against AutoDroid and AppAgent (lee2024mobilegptaugmentingllm pages 8-9). Key results show MobileGPT achieves near-perfect step accuracy (98.75%) during warm-start (lee2024mobilegptaugmentingllm pages 10-11), with memory hit rates varying by app (e.g., Telegram 98.6%, Gmail 70.6%) (lee2024mobilegptaugmentingllm pages 11-12). Warm-start latency and cost are reduced by 62.5% and 68.8% over baseline (lee2024mobilegptaugmentingllm pages 11-12). Its screen classification achieved only 3 false positives and no false negatives across 269 app screens (lee2024mobilegptaugmentingllm pages 7-8). MobileGPT achieves 82.5% success rate in warm-start and 82.16% overall, outperforming baselines on its dataset (lee2024mobilegptaugmentingllm pages 8-9). The system offloads computation to servers, with average power consumption of 330.38 mW on mobile devices (lee2024mobilegptaugmentingllm pages 11-12).
